Mary J. Blige, Cher and Ozzy Osbourne Are Rock & Roll Hall of Fame Inductees


Cher, Mary J. Blige, Ozzy Osbourne and Q-Tip by Dimitrios Kambouris, Derek White, Frazer Harrison and Paul Morigi

During 'Rock & Roll Hall of Fame Night' on 'American Idol,' the latest inductees into the prestigious Rock & Roll Hall of Fame were revealed. Among them are icons like Mary J. Blige, Cher, and Ozzy Osbourne. The announcement, made by Ryan Seacrest and Lionel Richie, included other notable artists such as the Dave Matthews Band, Foreigner, Peter Frampton, Kool & The Gang, and A Tribe Called Quest. In addition to the Performers category, which celebrates artists who've significantly influenced rock and roll, recipients of other awards were unveiled, including the Musical Influence Award for Alexis Korner, John Mayall, and Big Mama Thornton, and the Musical Excellence Award for Jimmy Buffett, MC5, Dionne Warwick, and Norman Whitfield. Suzanne de Passe will be honored with the Ahmet Ertegun Award for her contributions to the industry. The induction ceremony is scheduled for October 19 at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland, streaming on Disney+ and later airing on ABC. Eligible for induction this year, Cher, Foreigner, Peter Frampton, and Kool & the Gang are being recognized during their first year of eligibility. The ceremony follows the induction of 13 other music legends in 2023, including Kate Bush, Sheryl Crow, Missy Elliot, George Michael, Willie Nelson, and Rage Against the Machine.

In a nail-biter, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander leads Thunder to Game 1 victory over Pelicans


Shai Gilgeous-Alexander by Herbert Jones

In a thrilling Game 1 of the first-round Western Conference playoff series, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander propelled the Oklahoma City Thunder to a narrow 94-92 win over the New Orleans Pelicans. Gilgeous-Alexander's stellar performance, including 28 points and a crucial go-ahead basket in the final minute, was pivotal in securing the victory for the top-seeded Thunder.

Despite a late surge from the Pelicans led by Trey Murphy III and CJ McCollum, who scored 21 and 20 points respectively, the Thunder managed to hold on for their first home playoff victory since 2019. Jalen Williams and Chet Holmgren also made significant contributions for the Thunder, with Williams scoring 19 points and Holmgren adding 15 points, 11 rebounds, and five assists.

The absence of Zion Williamson due to a strained left hamstring impacted the Pelicans' offensive firepower, contributing to their narrow loss. The game was filled with tense moments, with Gilgeous-Alexander's clutch play ultimately making the difference. His calmness under pressure and ability to deliver when it mattered most earned him praise from fans and teammates.

The victory sets the stage for an exciting playoff series between the Thunder and the Pelicans, highlighting the intensity and drama of postseason basketball.
